Zero rational-approximation surface in reality (no `Pade`, no `RationalApprox`, no general `ContinuedFraction` evaluator — only `betaContinuedFraction` baked into `prob/copula/studentt.go:195`); a ~430 LOC day-1 PR (`rational/` package: T0 direct Padé from Taylor + T1 robust Padé via Gonnet-Güttel-Trefethen 2013 SVD + T2 continued-fraction evaluator + matrix-exponential glue) saturates 4 R-MUTUAL 3/3 pins and supplies the primitive that slot 297 (asymptotic), slot 298 (special functions), slot 315 (IIR), and a future linalg `Expm` all want.

## Findings

- **Total surface absent.** Grep across reality for `Pade|RationalApprox|ContinuedFraction|Continued.*Fraction|RobustPade|expm|MatrixExp` returns: (a) the `betaContinuedFraction` helper at `prob/copula/studentt.go:195` (a hand-coded Lentz modified-CF for the regularized incomplete-beta — single-purpose, not exposed); (b) `prob/distributions.go:57-77` Acklam (2004) rational approximation for the inverse normal (callable as `prob.NormalQuantile`, but the rational coefficients are inlined, not a generic Padé builder); (c) review-side mentions only. **No Padé builder, no general continued-fraction evaluator, no matrix-exponential.**
- **Slot 297 (asymptotic) reserved Padé as T7 (~50 LOC `asymptotic/pade.go`)** — see `reviews/overnight-400/agents/297-new-asymptotic-analysis.md:46`. Slot 297 sketches it as a one-shot Toeplitz solve; **this slot deepens it** into a proper sub-package because Padé has consumers far beyond asymptotics (matrix exponential, IIR analog→digital design, rational transfer-function fitting, special-function asymptotics outside Taylor radius, Borel-Laplace resummation) and deserves its own home with three evaluation modes (P/Q form, continued fraction, matrix form).
- **Slot 297 already owns Borel summation (T5).** This slot owns the **standalone rational tools** that Borel composes through; we should keep the package boundary clean — `rational/` ships generic Padé/CF, `asymptotic/` calls `rational.Pade` then runs the Laplace-back integral. No duplication.
- **calculus/ has no rational-approx code** (`calculus/calculus.go` is 274 LOC: `NumericalDerivative`, `NumericalGradient`, `TrapezoidalRule`, `SimpsonsRule`, `GaussLegendre`, `MonteCarloIntegrate`, `Bisection`, `NewtonRaphson`). No `Romberg`, no `Aitken`, no Padé.
- **signal/ has no transfer-function code.** Slot 315 (`reviews/overnight-400/agents/315-dive-iir-design.md`) plans to ship `signal/iir.go` with bilinear/prewarping/biquad. Padé enters slot 315 territory because **the bilinear transform is exactly the order-(1,1) Padé approximant of `e^{sT}`** (`(1+sT/2)/(1−sT/2)`), and higher-order **diagonal Padé approximants give Tustin-with-prewarping at minimum-phase IIR design**. So `rational.Pade` is a standalone primitive that slot 315 can consume.
- **linalg has `LUDecompose`/`LUSolve`/`CholeskySolve`** (`linalg/decompose.go:21,103,316`) but **no `Expm` matrix exponential**. The Higham (2008) scaling-and-squaring with [13/13]-Padé is the canonical algorithm — it requires (a) `rational.PadeOfExp` (closed-form, doesn't even need a Toeplitz solve since coefficients are explicit b_k = (2m−k)!m!/((2m)!k!(m−k)!)) and (b) one matrix linear-solve `Q⁻¹P` — both already available.
- **No competitor in pure-Go MIT zero-dep land.** Gonum has `mat.Exp` via Golub-Van Loan (Apache 2.0, depends on BLAS). `mpmath` (BSD-3 Python) has `pade()` and `padeapprox()`. Julia has `RobustPade.jl` (MIT). MATLAB has built-in `pade` (Symbolic Toolbox proprietary), `expm` built-in. **Pure-Go MIT zero-dep Padé/robust-Padé/CF/matrix-Padé library = ABSENT.** Modest but real moat; very high research-utility.
- **Toeplitz structure of Padé linear system.** Given Taylor coefficients c₀, c₁, …, c_{m+n}, the Padé denominator b₁..b_n satisfies a Toeplitz system T·b = −c, where T is the lower-triangular Toeplitz built from c_m, c_{m−1}, …, c_{m−n+1}. **Reality already has `LUSolve`** — just build the matrix and call it (n typically ≤ 13 so fancy Levinson-Trench-Zohar O(n²) Toeplitz solver isn't needed; O(n³) LU is fine for canonical Padé use cases).
- **Robust Padé (Gonnet-Güttel-Trefethen 2013, SIAM Review 55:101-117).** Replaces the Toeplitz solve with an SVD-based rank-revealing solve. Detects spurious zero-pole "Froissart doublets" by examining trailing singular values and reduces (m,n) until all singular values exceed a tolerance. **Algorithm 1** of the paper is ~30 lines of MATLAB. Once shipped, robustly handles series with rounding-noise where naive Padé blows up.
- **Continued-fraction evaluation is more numerically stable than P/Q form** when poles cluster. Wall (1948) "Analytic Theory of Continued Fractions" gives the conversion from a Padé table column to a Stieltjes/Jacobi continued fraction; Bauer's `eta`-algorithm and Wynn's `epsilon`-algorithm are the practical recursive extractors. Per Numerical Recipes §5.2, evaluate via modified Lentz (Thompson-Barnett 1986) — same algorithm `prob/copula/studentt.go:195` already implements for the beta CF. **Generalize that hand-coded helper into a public `rational.EvalCF` and reuse it.**
- **Matrix Padé.** Higham's `expm` algorithm: scale `A` by 2⁻ˢ until `‖2⁻ˢA‖₁ < θ_{13} = 5.371920351148152` (Higham 2005 Table 2.3), evaluate [13/13] Padé `r(2⁻ˢA) = q(2⁻ˢA)⁻¹·p(2⁻ˢA)` using **Paterson-Stockmeyer** matrix-polynomial multiplication (4 matrix products instead of 13 for [13/13]), then square s times. Total cost ~ (s+6) matrix multiplies. **180 LOC ceiling** with explicit b_k coefficients.
- **AAA algorithm (Nakatsukasa-Sète-Trefethen 2018, SIAM J. Sci. Comp. 40:A1494-A1522).** Greedy adaptive Antoulas-Anderson rational approximation in barycentric form — not a Padé method (no Taylor input; works from function samples) but the **modern best-in-class** for rational fits to functions on real/complex sets. Composes with `rational.Pade` cleanly: AAA fits from samples, Padé fits from Taylor; both produce the same data structure (`(num, den)` polynomial pair or barycentric weights). **Defer to T6** — research-grade, ~250 LOC.

## Concrete recommendations

1. **T0 — `rational/pade.go` direct Padé from Taylor (~120 LOC, day-1).** Signature `Pade(taylor []float64, m, n int) (num, den []float64, err error)`. Build the Toeplitz lower-triangular n×n matrix `T[i][j] = taylor[m+i−j]` for `j ≤ i`, RHS `-taylor[m+1..m+n]`, call existing `linalg.LUDecompose`+`LUSolve` to obtain denominator coefficients (b₀=1 by convention), then convolve with Taylor to get numerator coefficients a₀..a_m. Length-checks `len(taylor) ≥ m+n+1`. Returns error on singular Toeplitz (matched-zero-pole case → tell user to call `RobustPade`). **R-MUTUAL 3/3 PIN:** Padé[4,4] of e^x at x=0 has closed-form `b_k = (8−k)!·4!/(8!·k!·(4−k)!)` via Higham 2005 — (i) compute via `Pade(taylor[:9], 4, 4)`, (ii) compute via the closed formula, (iii) evaluate the rational against `math.Exp` for x ∈ {0.1, 0.5, 1.0} — three-way agreement to 1e-13. Ship as golden file `rational/testdata/pade_exp_4_4.json`.

2. **T1 — `rational/robust_pade.go` Gonnet-Güttel-Trefethen 2013 SVD-based robust Padé (~150 LOC, day-1).** Signature `RobustPade(taylor []float64, m, n int, tol float64) (num, den []float64, mEff, nEff int, err error)` returning the **effective** orders after spurious-pole reduction. Algorithm 1 of GGT 2013: form (n+1) × (m+n+1) "Toeplitz" matrix C, run thin SVD via existing `linalg` (slot 097 noted: SVD not yet in linalg — **dependency**, though for small (m,n)≤13 a Jacobi-rotation SVD is ~80 LOC standalone), find smallest σ; if σ < tol·σ_max, reduce n until rank holds. Reqd dep: a small SVD or QR-based rank-revealer in linalg. **R-MUTUAL 3/3 PIN:** Series `f(x) = log(1+x)·(1+x²/3)` perturbed by 1e-13 random noise → naive `Pade(.., 5, 5)` produces a Froissart doublet inside |x|<1 (verifiable by zero-of-numerator vs zero-of-denominator near-coincidence within 1e-6); `RobustPade(.., 5, 5, 1e-10)` returns `(mEff, nEff) = (4, 4)` or `(5, 4)`, no doublet. Three legs: noisy-Padé fails / robust-Padé succeeds / clean-Padé matches robust-Padé.

3. **T2 — `rational/contfrac.go` continued-fraction evaluator (~80 LOC, day-1).** Two functions. `(a) PadeToCF(num, den []float64) (a0 float64, a, b []float64, err error)` converts numerator/denominator coefficients to a generalized continued fraction `a0 + b1/(a1 + b2/(a2 + …))` via the qd-algorithm (Rutishauser 1954, "Der Quotienten-Differenzen-Algorithmus", Z. angew. Math. Phys. 5:233-251). `(b) EvalCF(a0 float64, a, b []float64, x float64, maxIter int, tol float64) float64` evaluates by modified Lentz (Thompson-Barnett 1986) — **factor out** the inlined `betaContinuedFraction` at `prob/copula/studentt.go:195-228` and have copula call this public helper; identical math, public API. **R-MUTUAL 3/3 PIN:** for [4/4] Padé of e^x, evaluate at x ∈ {0.1, 0.5, 1.0, 2.0} via (i) direct `num/den` polynomial division, (ii) `EvalCF` after `PadeToCF`, (iii) `math.Exp` — three-way agreement to 1e-13.

4. **T3 — `linalg/expm.go` matrix exponential via [13/13]-Padé scaling-and-squaring (~150 LOC, day-2).** Signature `Expm(A []float64, n int, out []float64) error`. Higham (2008) Algorithm 10.20: compute `‖A‖₁`, choose s=⌈log₂(‖A‖₁/θ_{13})⌉, scale `A_s = A/2^s`, evaluate `[13/13]` Padé using Paterson-Stockmeyer `(A², A⁴, A⁶)` precomputation (b_k explicit, no Toeplitz solve needed), solve `Q·R = P` via existing `LUDecompose/LUSolve` to get `r(A_s)`, square s times. **R-MUTUAL 3/3 PIN:** for diagonal `A = diag(λ₁,…,λ_n)`, `Expm(A) = diag(e^{λ_k})` exactly via stdlib `math.Exp`; for nilpotent `J` (Jordan block size ≤ 5), `Expm(J) = I + J + J²/2 + … + J^{n-1}/(n-1)!` exactly; for symmetric `A = QΛQᵀ`, `Expm(A) = Q·diag(e^{Λ_k})·Qᵀ` — three legs computable without Padé and validating Padé. Composes existing `linalg.LUDecompose`. No new deps.

5. **T4 — `rational/aaa.go` AAA rational approximation from samples (~250 LOC, day-3 / frontier).** Nakatsukasa-Sète-Trefethen 2018. Greedy support-point selection, barycentric form, SVD-based weight solve. Different input modality from Padé (function values vs Taylor coefficients), useful for fitting empirical data and meromorphic functions on contours. **Defer.** The 40-line MATLAB reference is in the AAA paper; full robust port with greedy point selection and convergence detection is closer to 250 LOC in Go. Document as the "samples-side complement" to T0/T1's Taylor-side.

## Cross-link consumers

- **Slot 297 (`agents/297-new-asymptotic-analysis.md` T7).** Replace its inline `~50 LOC asymptotic/pade.go` with `import "github.com/davly/reality/rational"` + `rational.Pade(...)`. Slot 297 keeps Borel-Laplace integral, this slot owns the rational machinery. Cleaner.
- **Slot 298 (special functions).** ₂F₁ outside its Taylor radius |z|<1 is standardly evaluated via the [m/m]-Padé of the truncated hypergeometric series (mpmath strategy). `rational.RobustPade` is the right primitive.
- **Slot 315 (`agents/315-dive-iir-design.md` IIR design).** Bilinear transform `s = (2/T)·(z−1)/(z+1)` is exactly the [1/1]-Padé of the natural-log/exp pair. Slot 315 can compose `rational.Pade` for higher-order matched-Z transforms (Tustin-with-prewarping is a [2/2] case).
- **Slot 297 T5 BorelSum.** Borel summation typically struggles when the Borel transform itself has finite radius of convergence; **conformal-Padé** (replace the Borel series with its Padé approximant before Laplace-back) is the standard rescue (Ecalle 1981, Costin 2009 ch. 6). Direct consumer of `rational.RobustPade`.
- **Future linalg `Expm` (slot 097 noted absence).** Direct T3 deliverable; consumed by control/ for state-transition matrices `Φ(t) = e^{At}` (slot 052 mentioned absence), by chaos/ for variational-flow Jacobian propagation, by orbital/ for linearized two-body dynamics.
- **Slot 116 (prob-numerics).** Many tail-probability calculations use Padé approximants (Cornish-Fisher inversion, saddlepoint normalization). `prob/copula/studentt.go`'s hand-coded CF is the only existing example; T2 makes it reusable.
